Stance System

EQL replaces classic disciplines with a modern stance system.

What Are Stances?

In classic EverQuest, classes had "Disciplines" — timed abilities with cooldowns. EQL replaces these entirely with Stances, persistent modes that change how your character fights. Stances have no cooldown to activate but cost endurance while active. Only one stance can be active at a time.

The Five Core Stances

Stance Strategy by Combo

Dave's Combo (Bard/Paladin/Enchanter)

  • Defensive — Default. You have Paladin, so Defensive stance + plate = 50% damage reduction makes you an incredibly durable tank.
  • Offensive — Use when your charmed pet is tanking, or when fighting trivial content.
  • Balanced — Use for general soloing when endurance conservation matters.

Christy's Combo (Cleric/Necromancer/Wizard)

  • Balanced — Default. As a caster trio, melee stances are less relevant.
  • Defensive — Emergency survival if you draw aggro (Cleric in plate can survive).
  • Offensive — Only useful if you're using weapons (rare for pure casters).

🔑 Strategy Skill

EQL adds a Strategy skill that improves stance efficiency. Leveling this skill reduces endurance cost of your active stance. Train it early.

Key Takeaways

  • Stances replace classic disciplines entirely — no more timed cooldown abilities
  • Only one stance active at a time
  • Endurance cost while active — use Balanced when not actively fighting
  • Paladin + Defensive = excellent tanking core for Dave's combo
  • Caster combo primarily uses Balanced stance

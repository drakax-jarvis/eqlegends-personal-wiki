Ritual Travel Guide

How to unlock permanent teleportation on any character by briefly leveling a Druid or Wizard.

🎯 Your Plan

Dave: Bard (primary) + Paladin + Enchanter → but first, briefly add Wizard as a secondary class to unlock portal Rituals, then swap it out.

Christy: Cleric (primary) + Necromancer + Wizard → but first, briefly add Druid as a secondary class to unlock travel Rituals, then swap it out.

⚠️ Important Correction — Gate + Bind Affinity Already Covered

Dave: Your Enchanter learns Gate at level 4 and Bind Affinity at level 12. You do NOT need Wizard for these basics.

Christy: Your Cleric learns Gate at level 5 and Bind Affinity at level 14. You do NOT need Druid for these basics.

The Ritual donor classes are only needed for actual teleportation (moving to other zones) and run speed — not for Gate or Bind.

How Rituals Work

From the EQL class combo builder: "Rituals — keep Druid/Wizard ports even when the class isn't slotted."

From the community: "You can level a Druid or Wizard up to unlock the self ports. Then they become ritual spells available to you no matter your class combo."

Key mechanics:

  • Ritual spells appear in the Actions window (default keybind: L)
  • You must be out of combat and in a resting state to use them
  • Once you learn a teleport spell by having that class slotted, it stays available via Rituals forever — even after swapping that class out
  • This applies to: Wizard Common Gate + Teleports, Druid Spirit of Wolf + Circles + Succor
  • Note: Evacuate is Wizard level 57 (Kunark era) — not available at launch

Strategy — The Ritual Rush

Since Gate and Bind Affinity come from your existing classes, the Ritual donor classes are specifically for inter-zone travel and run speed:

  1. Create both characters with their intended primary classes (Dave=Bard, Christy=Cleric).
  2. Your second class at creation should be your Ritual donor (Dave=Wizard, Christy=Druid).
  3. Level to the Ritual target levels below — this takes a few hours of light grinding.
  4. At level 10, pick your third class as your intended main (Dave=Paladin or Enchanter, Christy=Necromancer or Wizard).
  5. Once you have the Ritual spells learned, swap out the Ritual donor for your final class in a major city.
  6. The travel spells remain available via the Actions window as Rituals.

📋 One-Time Process

This is a one-time setup. Once you unlock Rituals, every character you ever play on that server benefits. Do it early before you get invested in your main combo.

💡 Dave's Minimum Target: Level 24 (Common Gate)

Common Gate at L24 is the prize — instant self-port to West Commonlands, the central travel hub. You already get Gate + Bind Affinity from your Enchanter at levels 4 and 12, so Wizard's value is entirely about inter-zone teleportation. If you don't want to invest the time, skip Wizard entirely — you still have Gate + Bind + Bard Selo's run speed.

💡 Christy's Minimum Target: Level 14 (Spirit of Wolf)

Spirit of Wolf at L14 gives the whole duo permanent run speed — this is massive for leveling speed. Bard Selo's Accelerando (L1) serves the same purpose, but they stack differently — Selo's is song-based, SoW is a buff. Having both means one of you can buff the other if your classes change. If you push to 29 for Circle ports, Christy becomes your group's dedicated transporter.

⚠️ Important Notes

  • Your primary class locks at level 11 — so Bard and Cleric should stay as primary from the start
  • The third class unlocks at level 10 — you can use it to help with the Ritual leveling
  • Class swapping is done in major cities — plan your route to have access to one
  • Once you swap out the Ritual class, its combat abilities go away — only the Ritual spells persist
  • Bard Selo's Accelerando (L1) + Druid Spirit of Wolf (L14) stack? Verify in beta — they may overwrite each other
  • Wizard Gate + Bard Selo's = you can set bind anywhere and zoom back at high speed

Monk

The unarmed master — peerless melee DPS, puller, and avoidance tank.

🥋 Monk

Role: Melee DPS / Puller / Off-Tank / Scout

Leather ArmorHand-to-HandPullFeign Death

Core Description

Monks are the masters of martial arts, dealing devastating melee damage with their bare hands or hand-to-hand weapons. They are cloth-wearing warriors whose avoidance-based defense — combined with the unique ability Feign Death — makes them the best pullers in the game.

Monks are defined by their high damage output through the Flying Kick combat skill and dual-wield capabilities. Their Mend ability allows them to heal themselves once per tick, providing significant self-sufficiency. The combo of Feign Death (available at L5) and Mend makes Monks exceptionally durable despite wearing only leather armor — they can fake death to drop aggro, mend, stand up, and re-engage without needing a healer's attention.

🥋 EQL Monk Change

In EQL, Monks retain their identity as the premier puller and melee DPS class. Feign Death is available at L5 (same as classic) and has a slightly higher success rate in EQL. Monks also gain the Feign Death Critical passive — when feigning successfully in combat, their next attack within 6 seconds is a guaranteed critical hit. Flying Kick is available at L1 and auto-upgrades at key levels. Mend has a reduced cooldown, increasing Monk self-sufficiency.

Stat Priorities

  1. Strength (STR): Melee damage and carry capacity. STR directly increases Monk DPS through all hand-to-hand attacks and kick skills.
  2. Stamina (STA): HP pool. Despite avoidance tanking, Monks need HP for survivability when things go wrong and to fuel Mend effectiveness.
  3. Dexterity (DEX): Affects weapon procs and skill success rates. DEX also improves Feign Death success rate in EQL.
  4. Agility (AGI): Avoidance tanking. AGI increases dodge chance and AC from leather armor. Useful but lower priority than raw damage stats.

EQL-Specific Monk Changes

  • Feign Death at L5 — same as classic, with improved success rate in EQL
  • Feign Death Critical — successful FD in combat guarantees a critical hit on next attack within 6 seconds. This is a massive DPS boost and changes pulling strategy.
  • Flying Kick at L1 — available immediately, auto-upgrades to Dragon Punch (L22), Eagle Strike (L35)
  • Mend at L1 — reduced cooldown compared to classic, with auto-upgrades at L20 and L40
  • Dual Wield at L1 — Monks dual-wield from level 1 with hand-to-hand weapons
  • Monk AAs confirmed: Improved Mend, Feign Death Mastery, Critical Strike, Avoidance Mastery, Dragon Stance
  • Beastlord stance hybrid — Monks in EQL can adopt a beastlord-like stance that sacrifices some defense for increased attack speed and damage
  • Sneak — available to Monk (shared with Bard, Ranger, Rogue, Shadow Knight)
  • Tracking — Monk has tracking ability in EQL (shared with Bard, Ranger, Druid)
  • Optimal puller — Feign Death + Sneak + tracking makes Monk the premier pulling class for any group

Monk AA Priority

  1. Feign Death Mastery — increases FD success rate and reduces reuse timer. Critical for pulling reliability.
  2. Critical Strike — improves melee critical hit chance. Direct DPS increase.
  3. Improved Mend — increases Mend heal amount and reduces cooldown further
  4. Avoidance Mastery — increases dodge and parry chance
  5. Dragon Stance — increases kick damage and attack speed
  6. Dual Wield Expertise — increases off-hand attack chance and double attack rate

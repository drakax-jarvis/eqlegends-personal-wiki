Brian's Combo — Shadow Knight / Shaman / Monk

The classic melee trifecta — debuff, tank, and murder. Ranked #1 standard non-charm combo by the Class Combo Builder.

🎯 Combo Rating: Top Tier (S-Class)

Scored as #1 Standard Non-Charm Combo in the Class Combo Builder. The Hero's Journey community also ranks SK/Shaman/Monk as the single highest-scoring combination in the game. Shadow Knight tanks and lifetaps, Shaman slows and heals, Monk delivers the best melee DPS in classic EQ.

How the Combo Works

This is a melee-focused pressure build with no reliance on mana for primary damage output.

  • Shadow Knight — Main tank. Plate armor, lifetaps for self-healing, disease-based spells, snares, and Harm Touch (massive burst damage). SK provides Feign Death as a backup pull tool.
  • Shaman — Slows + heals + buffs. Shaman is the best slow class in the game (Tigir's Insects line — attack speed debuff). Provides stat buffs (Strength, Stamina), heal-over-time (Regrowth/Chloroplast), and debuffs (Malo line for resist reduction). Wears chain but SK provides plate for the character.
  • Monk — Melee DPS + pull. Monk delivers the highest sustained melee DPS in classic EQ. Feign Death for safe pulling and splitting camps. Mend (self-heal). Flying kick for extra damage. Can assist SK on pulls with FD splitting.

Stat Priorities

  1. Strength (STR): Melee damage for Monk + SK. Shaman gets STR from buffs.
  2. Stamina (STA): HP for all three. SK tanks hits, Monk is in melee range, Shaman needs to survive.
  3. Wisdom (WIS): Shaman mana pool. Only the Shaman uses mana for spells.
  4. Dexterity (DEX): Reduces fizzle for Shaman, affects weapon procs for SK and Monk.
  5. Agility (AGI): AC for Monk (who uses avoidance over mitigation). Shaman buff provides AGI.

Leveling Strategy

This combo excels at pulling singles and burning them down. Shaman slows the target (Tigir's), SK tanks with lifetaps, Monk DPS from behind.

  • 1-10: Whatever the primary gives you. All three classes level simultaneously.
  • 10-20: Unrest courtyard. Monk pulls with FD, SK tanks, Shaman slows + heals.
  • 20-30: Mistmoore graveyard / Upper Guk. Shaman slow + SK tanking makes pulls very safe.
  • 30-40: Lower Guk (Crusader/Assassin area). Your combo's sweet spot — SK holds aggro with lifetaps, Shaman slows everything, Monk burns. Guk death-touch traps are manageable with double FD.
  • 40-50: Sol B (cobalts → Ephriti) / City of Mist / Kedge Keep. SK harm touch burst + Shaman debuffs + Monk sustained DPS.
  • 46+: Plane of Hate / Plane of Fear. SK tanks planar mobs, Shaman keeps slow up, Monk delivers DPS.

Duo & Group Strategy

Duo with a Healer (Cleric or Druid)

This is a very strong duo. With a Cleric or Druid healing, your Shaman can focus entirely on slows and debuffs while the Monk burns DPS. The Cleric provides superior single-target heals and resurrection safety. The Druid provides group heals, damage shields, and ports. The SK's lifetaps supplement healing so the duo healer rarely has to panic heal.

  • Let the healer handle reactive heals. Your Shaman casts slow and debuffs only.
  • SK should use lifetaps after the initial aggro is set — this frees the healer's mana.
  • Monk FD-pulls single targets. Healer sits and meditates during pulls.
  • Ideal camps: Unrest (10-20), Mistmoore (20-30), Lower Guk (30-40), Sol B (40-50).

Duo with a Caster (Wizard or Necromancer)

The SK holds aggro while the Shaman provides slows and STR/STA buffs. The caster burns the slowed target down. The SK's snare (Heat Blood) lets the caster kite if needed. With a Necromancer, double Feign Death gives incredible pull safety.

  • Necromancer sends pet + dots, SK holds aggro via lifetaps, Shaman slows + buffs.
  • Wizard nukes hard on slowed targets. Shaman Avatar buff (+STR/STA) helps the SK tank longer.
  • Monk pulls via FD or tags with throwing weapons. Feign Death cancels aggro on adds.
  • Ideal camps: Oasis (10-20), South Karana (20-30), Sol A (30-40), City of Mist (40-50).

Full 3-Class Playstyle

When all three classes are leveled and the combo is online, the rotation is smooth and rhythmic:

  1. Pre-fight: Shaman casts stat buffs (STR/STA/AGI), Spirit of Wolf, and Regrowth. SK activates defensive buffs. Monk meditates/stands ready.
  2. Pull: Monk FD-splits the camp. Tags one target, feigns death, stands and engages. For single pulls, Monk simply pulls with throwing weapons and the SK grabs aggro.
  3. Engage: SK opens with Siphon Strength (STR debuff + aggro) or Disease Cloud. Shaman lands Tigir's Insects (slow) — this is priority #1. Monk moves behind the mob for backstab-position Flying Kick.
  4. Sustain: Shaman keeps slow up, casts HoT on SK, debuffs with Malo line. SK cycles lifetaps for self-healing and holds aggro. Monk maintains consistent DPS from behind.
  5. Burn phase: SK pops Harm Touch on the pull for a massive opening burst. Shaman drops Avatar on the SK for more tank DPS. Monk activates any offensive discipline.
  6. Between pulls: Shaman re-buffs and meditates. SK self-heals with lifetaps. Monk meditates or bandages. Zero downtime since Monk needs no mana and SK uses lifetaps.

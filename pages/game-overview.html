EverQuest Legends — Game Overview

Everything you need to know about the reimagined classic MMORPG.

What Is EverQuest Legends?

EverQuest Legends (EQL) is a reimagined version of classic EverQuest from its original 1999 release, developed by Game Jawn in partnership with Daybreak Games. It features the same classic art style, graphics, zones, spell effects, loot, and music — but with a revolutionary multiclassing system and modern quality-of-life improvements.

📅 Key Dates

Pre-Order Beta: July 1–21, 2026 (servers wiped at end)
Full Launch: July 28, 2026
Pre-Orders Open: June 16, 2026 — includes beta access, name reservation, and "The Legend" title.

The Multiclassing System

The defining feature of EQL: you build a character by selecting up to 3 classes. All three classes' spells, abilities, skills, and passives are active simultaneously. With 16 classes and 560 possible combinations, the variety is immense.

  • Primary class: Race-locked at character creation, locks at level 11
  • Second class: Free choice at creation
  • Third class: Unlocked at level 10
  • Class swapping: Non-primary classes can be swapped freely in major cities
  • Effective level: The lowest of your three class levels
  • Armor: Whatever your best armor class allows across all three classes

Available Races (15 at Launch)

Race requirements have been removed from most items. Additional races beyond starting options unlocked through achievements (first unlock token at level 50).

  • Good: Human, Barbarian, Half-Elf, Wood Elf, High Elf, Dwarf, Gnome, Erudite, Halfling, Froglok, Kerran
  • Evil: Dark Elf, Ogre, Troll, Iksar

Quality of Life Changes

  • No bard twisting — songs activate once and sustain automatically
  • Always meditating — no need to sit; only sit to memorize spells
  • No corpse runs — respawn with all gear
  • No XP loss on death
  • No hell levels — smooth XP curve
  • No race/class XP modifiers
  • No coin weight
  • Three default UI skins + custom XML support
  • Map overlay with NPC tracking
  • Cast bars, extended target window, attack swing timer
  • Dragon's Hoard — 125 slot extra bank
  • Tradeskill Depot — 5000 slots, auto-pulls for combines
  • Personal loot only
  • 4K support in progress

Content Structure

  • Launch zones: All classic zones on Antonica, Faydwer, and Odus (pre-Kunark)
  • Planes: Plane of Fear and Plane of Hate at launch (level 46 entry)
  • Groups: Up to 4 players
  • Raids: Up to 8 players in raid zones
  • Instances: Pick zones at launch; private solo instances coming post-launch
  • Difficulty: 5 tiers — Normal through Fused
  • Dungeon crawl mode: Bonus loot for completing objectives inside dungeons
  • Housing: Yes at launch (East Commonlands Tunnel); Guild Halls post-launch

Monetization

  • Upfront purchase + subscription at lower end of expectations
  • No Krono — not included in All-Access
  • Cash shop: Cosmetics and utility only — no power items

Technical Details

  • Minimum: Windows 7 SP1 64-bit, i7-4771/Athlon X4 840, 4GB RAM, GTX 780 Ti/HD 7970, 20GB
  • Recommended: Windows 10 64-bit, i5-9600K/Ryzen 5 3600X, 8GB RAM, GTX 1080/RX 5700
  • PC only at launch, no Steam at launch
  • True Box enforced — no multiboxing
  • One character per server (multiple servers available)

Difficulty System

Zones scale across 5 difficulty levels: D0 (Normal) through D4 (Fused). D2 is the sweet spot for grinding — it feels like classic EQ. Higher difficulty = better XP, loot, and base merge levels.

  • D4 (Fused) — Extreme difficulty. Best payout. Requires strong combos: priest + tank + slow (your Cleric + Paladin + Enchanter fits perfectly). Motes (for item upgrade) drop significantly more in D4 group raids. D4 raid bosses are soloable soon after 50 regardless of build.
  • D2 — Classic EQ feel. Mobs gain extra classes and spells at this tier.

Exaltations & Gear Customization

Exaltations replace vendor augments. They let you transfer focus effects, procs, click effects, worn effects, and appearances between items by upgrading items to unlock slots (+1 for focus, +2 for click, +3 for worn, +4 for proc). Haste cannot be Exaltated. See the full Exaltations Guide.

Economy & Trading

  • Most BiS gear is no-trade — attunes on receipt. Tradable items attune on first trade. No unattuners exist.
  • EC Tunnel — trading hub in East Commonlands with teleporters from every region.
  • Plat income: Most leveling zones drop enough plat for 2 classes' full spell lists as you level. Rathe Mountains hill giants (32-36) are best plat/hour.
  • Coins have no weight. A bazaar/player-market interface is planned post-launch.

Post-Launch Roadmap

From the Producer's Letter June 30, 2026 — Executive Producer Dave "Sysyphus" Youssefi:

  • Beta: Preorder Open Beta July 1–21, wiped July 21. Launch July 28.
  • Spell upgrades — New feature still being completed at beta launch.
  • Dungeon revamps — Continuing through beta and post-launch.
  • Player auction house — Planned post-launch (not ready at launch).
  • Housing — Post-launch roadmap item.
  • Nektropos Castle — Fall 2026 content update.
  • Kunark expansion — Winter 2026.

Full roadmap to be published after launch.

What's NOT in the Game

Krono, All-Access inclusion, Mercenaries, Guild Halls at launch, three simultaneous pets, multiboxing, MacroQuest/automation, Steam at launch, dyes, spell research tradeskill, vendor augments (replaced by Exaltations), Complete Heal (replaced by Promise Renewal), classic disc system (replaced by stances), alts at launch, coin weight, sitting to meditate, race restrictions on most items.
